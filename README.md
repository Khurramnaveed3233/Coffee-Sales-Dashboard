#  Coffee Sales Analysis Dashboard – Excel Business Intelligence

> **Role:** Data Analyst | **Tools:** Microsoft Excel · Pivot Tables · Dynamic Charts · Slicers | **Domain:** FMCG · Retail · Sales Analytics

---

##  Project Overview

Built an interactive Excel dashboard analyzing **coffee sales across 3 countries from 2019 to 2022** — covering time-based trends, geographic performance, customer segmentation, roast type preferences, and loyalty card impact. The dashboard enables sales leaders to **monitor performance trends, identify top customers, and optimize product mix and promotional strategy** for sustainable revenue growth.

---

##  Business Problem

Coffee sales leadership had no centralized tool to answer:

- Which **countries and regions** are over-relying on a single market?
- Who are the **top 5 high-value customers** and how do we retain them?
- How does **seasonality** affect demand and when should inventory be maximized?
- Which **roast types and pack sizes** drive the highest average order value?
- Are **loyalty card holders** generating more repeat purchases and larger baskets?

##  Key Findings

### 1. Total Sales Over Time (2019–2022)

- Sales fluctuate significantly across the 4-year period — showing **strong seasonal demand patterns**
- **Peaks in late 2019 and mid-2021** where monthly sales reached ~900 units
- Consistent volatility suggests demand is driven by **seasonality, promotions, and external market factors**
- **Takeaway:** Inventory and promotional planning must align with seasonal spikes to maximize revenue and prevent stockouts

### 2. Sales by Country

| Country | Sales | Status |
|---|---|---|
| United States | ~750 units | Top market — concentration risk |
| United Kingdom | ~400 units | Growth opportunity |
| Ireland | Lowest | Significant opportunity gap |

- **USA dominates** but heavy reliance on a single market creates concentration risk
- **UK and Ireland are significantly under-penetrated** — targeted campaigns can unlock growth
- **Takeaway:** Diversification into UK and Ireland is the highest-priority geographic expansion

### 3. Top 5 High-Value Customers

| Rank | Customer | Volume | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ingebart Hotchkin | ~200 units | Highest volume — VIP |
| 2 | Wallis Bernth | Significant | Strong buyer |
| 3 | Minny Chamberlayne | Moderate | Strong repeat buyer |
| 4 | Corrie Wass | Moderate | Consistent |
| 5 | Bertine Byrd | Lower | At-risk for churn |

- **Customer loyalty is highly concentrated** — a small group drives disproportionate revenue
- Top 5 customers represent a significant share of total sales — losing even one would be impactful
- **Takeaway:** VIP-level personalized offers and loyalty rewards are essential for top customer retention

### 4. Product Mix — Roast Types and Sizes

- **Dark and Medium roasts** drive the bulk of sales across all regions
- **Larger pack sizes (1.0 kg – 2.5 kg)** are associated with bulk buyers and loyal, high-frequency customers
- **Smaller sizes (0.2 kg – 0.5 kg)** attract trial and occasional buyers
- **Takeaway:** Promote larger packs to increase average order value and bundle Dark/Medium roasts in seasonal campaigns

### 5. Loyalty Card Impact

- Loyalty card filter reveals **repeat purchase patterns** and basket size differences
- Loyalty customers show **higher purchase frequency and larger average orders**
- Non-loyalty customers skew toward smaller, one-time purchases
- **Takeaway:** Expanding loyalty program enrollment is a direct lever for increasing customer lifetime value

---

##  Business Recommendations

### Market Expansion
| Action | Target | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Localized UK campaigns | UK market | Close gap from 400 → 600 units |
| Ireland market entry program | Ireland | Activate underserved demand |
| US market diversification | Reduce US concentration | Lower single-market risk |

### Customer Retention
- Launch **exclusive VIP offers** for top 5 customers with personalized roast bundles
- Develop a **referral program** to convert high-value customers into brand advocates
- Implement **early warning system** for customers showing purchase frequency decline

### Product Strategy
- Promote **1.0 kg – 2.5 kg pack sizes** in campaigns to increase basket size
- Bundle **Dark and Medium roasts** with loyalty card sign-up incentives
- Introduce **limited edition seasonal blends** during peak demand periods

### Seasonal Promotions
- **Q4 and mid-year** are peak periods — pre-load inventory and launch campaigns 4–6 weeks ahead
- Introduce **holiday bundles** to capture gift purchasing behavior in Q4
- Use historical monthly trend data to set **dynamic inventory targets** by month

---

##  Technical Approach

### Microsoft Excel
- **Pivot Tables** for multi-dimensional aggregation by country, customer, roast type, size, and time
- **Dynamic Charts** — line charts for time-series trends, bar charts for country and customer comparison
- **Slicers** for interactive filtering by roast type (Dark/Medium/Light), size (0.2–2.5 kg), and loyalty card status
- **Timeline Filter** for date-range selection across 2019–2022
- **Top-N Customer Analysis** using LARGE() and RANK() functions
- **Conditional Formatting** for quick identification of peak months and high-value customers
